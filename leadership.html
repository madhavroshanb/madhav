MUNoV: Building a more connected world

As CFO of an 8-person team of the Model United Nations of Vienna (MUNoV) 2025, we built the biggest MUN conference in Austria. The event generated Euro 40,000 in revenue, includinwith 4k sent off already and 15k earmarked for PROSA (Projekt Schule für Alle), a charity working to provide formal, recognised education to asylum seekers who have had to flee their homes, a neglected minority in Austria.

It was an extraordinary experience. We established lifelong relationships, debated issues passionately, and discussed how we could make the world a more peaceful, equitable place. I was honored to chair the Historical Security Council Session.

Student Council Representative

Responsibility, ethics and courage. An extraordinary year of experiences as part of my school's elected Student Council. Serving as the voice of your community and negotiating their demands with school administration, from shower curtains to on-campus phone usage, it was a learning experience like no other.

I used this as a springboard to help ensure a lasting legacy of accountability and transparency at my school through launching the school reform movement (SGRC). We've had meetings with and pitched to the board of directors, principal, PTA, and deputy principal, alongside running stalls at school events, and I'm credited as a co-director.

Here's a link to the website: https://sites.google.com/student.vis.ac.at/sgrc

We didn't stop there. After creating meaningful change at our school, we spoke with friends internationally and realised that these same issues tend to emerge across different schools, just because of the paradigm of student governments and elections. We're currently working on legally incorporating our organisiation, and franchising out, creating a branch in a chain of schools in prague, helping them set up their own student government(s), and leveraging our acquired knowledge to expand the impact that we can have across different contexts.
